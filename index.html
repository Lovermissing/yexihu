Yexi Lake of USTC

About Yexi Lake

Yexi Lake is located in the heart of the West Campus of the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). Originally a weed-covered depression, it was transformed in the 1980s during the construction of the West Campus when teachers and students rolled up their sleeves and worked together to dredge and renovate the wild pond, turning it into a major landscape feature of the campus.

The lake is lined with metasequoia trees and features a small island in the center, connected to the south bank by a wooden bridge. On the island stands the Yijian Pavilion (also known as the "One Heart Pavilion"). At the northeast corner of the lake lies the Fanghua Garden.

In spring, visitors can enjoy fishing by the lake, while in winter, flocks of wild ducks come to overwinter, and black swans often swim in its waters. It's a popular spot for both campus members and visitors to stroll and relax.

West Campus Library

The West Campus Library of the University of Science and Technology of China is the main library of USTC. It was put into use in September 1997 with an area of 18,587 square meters and reopened after space renovation in 2016.

Architectural Structure and Functional Zones

The main building floors 1-4 are the Future Learning Center, providing more than 20 different learning spaces including study rooms, Chinese newspaper reading rooms, book漂流 spaces, shared learning spaces, reservation seminar rooms, innovation and entrepreneurship studios, interview rooms, online course recording studios and academic lecture halls.

Floors 5-11 of the main building are areas such as reference book reading rooms, Chinese and foreign periodical rooms, and Chinese book weeding storage. The 12th floor of the main building is the Western Book Collection. The annex building floors 1-4 collect a total of 180,000 types and more than 400,000 volumes of social science and natural science books.
